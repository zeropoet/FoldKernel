# FoldKernel identity mark

The FoldKernel mark is a load-bearing plan rather than an illustration.

- The outer square is the protocol boundary: fixed, minimal, and stable.
- The inner square is the kernel: protected canonical state.
- The four corner folds are deterministic transformation paths.
- Their equal convergence expresses symmetry, reproducibility, and coherence.

## Family relationship

The mark uses the same 1024-unit field, `#111111` color, 32-unit stroke,
square line caps, and mitered joins as the Sovereign Standard mark. Its square
construction and centered modular logic allow it to sit beside Root Logos and
other marks in the family without becoming a variation of any one of them.

## Usage

Use `fold-kernel-mark.svg` as the archival source of truth. The fold strokes are
clipped exactly between the structural rings, producing clean joins at every
size without overlaps or projecting diagonal caps.

- `fold-kernel-mark.svg` — canonical accessible master in identity black
- `fold-kernel-mark-mono.svg` — compact pure-black production asset
- `fold-kernel-mark-reverse.svg` — compact white asset for dark fields

Preserve the clear space already present in the 1024-unit field. Do not round
corners, alter the relative stroke weights, fill the central kernel, or add a
background directly to the mark.
